How to Calculate Your CASPA GPA

Follow these steps to ensure your GPA calculation aligns with the CASPA GPA verifiy methodology.

1. Gather All Academic Transcripts

Collect transcripts from every college or university you have attended.

CASPA requires applicants to report all coursework, even if:
  • Credits were transferred
  • Courses were repeated
  • Grades did not count toward graduation

No course is excluded from CASPA GPA calculations.

2. Classify Each Course Correctly

CASPA separates GPA into specific categories. When entering courses into the calculator, select the appropriate classification:

  • Science GPA (BCP)
    Includes Biology, Chemistry, and Physics courses.
  • Non-Science GPA
    Includes Social Sciences, Humanities, Business, and elective courses.
  • Cumulative GPA
    Reflects the average of all courses combined, regardless of subject.

Accurate classification is essential for an accurate CASPA GPA estimate.

3. Enter Grades and Credit Hours

For each course, enter:

  • The letter grade
  • The credit hours earned

Important: CASPA does not use grade replacement.

If you repeated a course, both the original and repeated grades must be entered. Each attempt is included and averaged into your final GPA.

4. Convert Quarter Credits (If Applicable)

If your institution uses a quarter system, credits must be converted to semester hours before entry.

Conversion Formula: Quarter Hours * 0.667 = Semester Hours Example:

A 4.0 quarter-credit course equals 2.67 semester hours.

5. Review Your CASPA GPA Results

Once all courses are entered, the calculator will automatically display:

  • Total Quality Points: (Grade value * credit hours)
  • Total Semester Hours: Sum of all converted credits
  • Estimated CASPA GPA: Total Quality Points ÷ Total Semester Hours

This mirrors how CASPA verifies GPA during the application review process.

Frequently Asked Questions About CASPA GPA


Does CASPA calculate GPA?

Yes, CASPA calculates a cumulative GPA using all undergraduate and post-baccalaureate coursework you have ever completed. This includes courses from every institution attended, whether credits transferred or not.

CASPA converts each grade to its standardized 4.0 scale, multiplies it by the credit hours, and averages all attempts. Unlike many colleges, CASPA does not replace grades for repeated courses.

Does CASPA calculate science GPA?

Yes, CASPA calculates a separate science GPA (BCP) in addition to cumulative GPA. This GPA focuses on science coursework that is most relevant to PA school admissions.

Science GPA typically includes Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Anatomy, Physiology, Microbiology, and related laboratory courses. Many PA programs place greater emphasis on this GPA.

How do I calculate my GPA for CASPA?

To calculate your CASPA GPA accurately, you must enter every course ever taken, including course grades, credit hours, and course classifications.

This CASPA GPA calculator automates the process by converting grades to CASPA values, weighting them by credit hours, and generating cumulative, science, and non-science GPA results instantly.

Why is CASPA GPA lower than my college GPA?

CASPA GPA is often lower because it follows stricter calculation rules. CASPA includes all attempts of repeated courses and does not allow grade replacement.

In addition, CASPA standardizes grades across institutions and calculates a separate science GPA, which may reduce the overall average compared to a college GPA.

Is a 3.5 GPA too low for PA school?

A 3.5 GPA is not automatically too low, but competitiveness depends on the specific PA program. Many programs report average accepted GPAs around or above 3.5.

Admissions committees typically evaluate both cumulative GPA and science GPA alongside healthcare experience, test scores, and personal statements.

Which courses count for CASPA science GPA?

CASPA science GPA includes courses such as Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Anatomy, Physiology, Microbiology, and similar science-based subjects.

Accurate classification is critical, as science GPA plays a major role in PA school admissions decisions.

Can I use a spreadsheet with the CASPA GPA calculator?

You may organize your coursework in a spreadsheet, but manual calculations are prone to error and may not follow CASPA rules precisely.

This online CASPA GPA calculator ensures accuracy by automatically converting grades, weighting credit hours, and separating science and non-science GPA according to CASPA guidelines.

Can repeated courses improve my CASPA GPA?

No. CASPA includes all attempts of repeated courses when calculating GPA. Retaken courses do not replace earlier grades.

Both the original and repeated grades are averaged into your final CASPA GPA, which limits how much improvement retakes can provide.

How does CASPA handle transfer courses?

CASPA treats transfer courses the same as courses taken at your primary institution. Each course must be entered individually.

Grades are converted to CASPA’s standardized 4.0 scale and weighted by credit hours to ensure consistent GPA calculation across institutions.
