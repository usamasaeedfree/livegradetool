This is the same weighted GPA formula used across standardized academic evaluations, including LSAC's CAS GPA calculation.

How Does LSAC Calculate GPA?

The Law School Admission Council (LSAC) does not use your university GPA exactly as it appears on your transcript. Instead, LSAC recalculates your undergraduate GPA through the Credential Assembly Service (CAS) to ensure a fair and consistent evaluation for all applicants.

Now let's see how LSAC calculates GPA.

Transcript Review:

LSAC collects transcripts from each undergraduate institution you attended. This includes all courses completed before your first bachelor’s degree. That ensures a complete, accurate record of your academic history. After that, they review them.

Grade Standardization:

Since universities use different grading systems, LSAC converts each grade into a numerical value using its official LSAC Grade Conversion Table. This standardizes all grades to a common 4.33 GPA scale, allowing comparison across schools and education systems.

Weighted GPA Calculation:

Each converted grade is multiplied by its corresponding credit hours to calculate quality points. Courses with higher credit hours have a greater impact on your final GPA.

This weighted approach ensures that your GPA accurately reflects the importance of each course.

Final CAS GPA:

After processing all coursework, LSAC calculates the final CAS GPA by adding all grade points together and dividing the total by the number of credit hours, which is reported to law schools. This standardized GPA is used for:

  • Admissions decisions
  • Scholarship evaluations
  • Comparing applicants from different universities and grading systems

This standardized method allows law schools to fairly compare applicants from different universities and education systems. Your LSAC GPA may differ from your university GPA due to variations in grading scales and conversion rules. This ensures fairness, transparency, and consistency for all applicants.

LSAC GPA Scale (Official CAS Grade Conversion)

The LSAC GPA scale is used by the Law School Admission Council through its Credential Assembly Service (CAS) to standardize undergraduate grades for law school admissions. This conversion ensures that applicants from different universities and grading systems are evaluated fairly using a single academic standard.

Below is a clear, simplified overview of the LSAC GPA conversion scale. It reflects how letter grades and percentage-based grades are translated into LSAC's CAS GPA system.

LSAC GPA Value Letter Grade Typical Percentage Range Academic Meaning
4.33 A+ 98 – 100 Highest level of achievement
4.00 A 93 – 97 Excellent performance
3.67 A- 90 – 92 Very strong academic record
3.33 B+ 87 – 89 Above-average achievement
3.00 B 83 – 86 Good standing
2.67 B- 80 – 82 Acceptable performance
2.33 C+ 77 – 79 Below-average results
2.00 C 73 – 76 Minimum satisfactory level
1.67 C- 70 – 72 Marginal pass
1.33 D+ 67 – 69 Poor academic performance
1.00 D 63 – 66 Lowest passing grade
0.67 D- 60 – 62 Barely passing
0.00 F Below 60 Failing grade

Important: LSAC GPA values are calculated using attempted undergraduate coursework only. Graduate and master's-level grades are not included in CAS GPA calculations.

How LSAC Uses This GPA Scale

LSAC recalculates every applicant's undergraduate record using this scale as part of the CAS GPA calculation process. This means your LSAC CAS GPA may differ from your university GPA due to differences in grade forgiveness, repeated courses, and grading policies.

LSAC GPA vs. University GPA

Your university GPA is calculated by your institution using its own grading scale and academic policies, which can vary widely between colleges and countries.

Your LSAC GPA, on the other hand, is recalculated by LSAC through the Credential Assembly Service (CAS) using a standardized 4.0 scale.

Because LSAC applies uniform conversion rules to all transcripts, your LSAC GPA may differ from your university GPA. Law schools rely on the LSAC CAS GPA, not your original university GPA, when evaluating applications.

FAQs

What is a good LSAC GPA?

A good LSAC GPA depends on the selectivity of the law schools you are targeting. Based on historical admissions data and admissions committee guidance:

  • 3.7+ LSAC GPA: Highly competitive for top-tier (T14) U.S. law schools
  • 3.4 — 3.69: Strong for many Tier-1 and Tier-2 schools
  • 3.0 — 3.39: Acceptable for regional and mid-tier programs
  • Below 3.0: Challenging, but still viable with a strong LSAT and compelling profile

From an admissions perspective, LSAC GPA is treated as a standardized academic metric, which is why schools rely on it more heavily than your university GPA.

Why is my LSAC GPA lower than my undergraduate GPA?

This is one of the most common applicant concerns. In practice, LSAC GPA is often lower because LSAC:

  • Counts all attempted courses, including failures and repeats
  • Does not apply grade forgiveness or replacement policies
  • Includes withdrawals that carry credit
  • Converts grades using a uniform national scale, not institutional rules

Applicants frequently discover this difference only after transcript processing, which is why estimating your CAS GPA early is critical.

How does LSAC calculate A+?

LSAC assigns 4.33 grade points to an A+, which exceeds the standard 4.0 scale:

  • A+ = 4.33
  • A = 4.00
  • A- = 3.67

If your undergraduate institution issues A+ grades, LSAC may award the additional points. This can meaningfully increase your LSAC GPA, especially for applicants with multiple high-credit A+ courses.

Is a 3.7 GPA good for law school in Canada?

Yes. A 3.7 GPA is considered very strong for Canadian law schools, including:

  • University of Toronto
  • McGill University
  • University of British Columbia
  • Osgoode Hall Law School

While Canadian schools do not use LSAC GPA, a 3.7 undergraduate GPA combined with a competitive LSAT places applicants in a strong admissions position.

Does LSAC tell you your GPA?

Yes. Once your transcripts are processed, LSAC provides your official LSAC (CAS) GPA inside your Credential Assembly Service account. This is the GPA transmitted directly to law schools and used during admissions review.

Can your LSAC GPA be higher than your GPA?

Yes, though it is less common. LSAC GPA can be higher if:

  • Your transcript includes A+ grades (4.33)
  • Your institution capped GPA calculations at 4.0
  • Your grading system converts favorably under LSAC rules

Applicants with strong letter grades from strict grading institutions occasionally benefit from LSAC’s standardized conversion.

How to view, see, or find your LSAC GPA

To view your LSAC GPA:

  • Log in to your LSAC account
  • Open Credential Assembly Service (CAS)
  • Access your Academic Summary Report

This report displays your LSAC GPA, grade conversions, and academic breakdown exactly as law schools see it.

Where to find LSAC GPA

Your LSAC GPA is found exclusively in your CAS Academic Summary Report. It is not calculated by law schools or shown elsewhere. Any other GPA is unofficial.

Does LSAC GPA round up?

No. LSAC does not round GPAs. CAS GPAs are calculated to precise decimal values, and even small differences can matter at competitive law schools.

Which GPA does LSAC use?

LSAC uses a standardized CAS GPA, not your institutional GPA. This ensures consistent academic evaluation across applicants from different universities and grading systems.

LSAC GPA vs regular GPA / undergraduate GPA

  • Undergraduate GPA: Calculated by your university
  • LSAC GPA: Recalculated by LSAC using CAS rules

Admissions committees rely on LSAC GPA because it applies a uniform national standard.

How do I calculate my LSAC GPA / CAS GPA?

To calculate your LSAC GPA:

  • Convert each grade to LSAC grade points
  • Multiply grade points by credit hours
  • Add all grade points together
  • Divide by total attempted credit hours

This mirrors the same process LSAC uses internally when generating your CAS GPA.

Is LSAC GPA weighted?

No. LSAC GPA is not weighted for honors, AP, or course difficulty. All courses are treated equally based on grade and credit hours.

Is LSAC GPA out of 4.33?

Yes. LSAC GPA can exceed 4.0 because A+ grades are worth 4.33. This applies only if your institution issues A+ grades.

Does LSAC GPA include graduate school or master’s degrees?

No. LSAC GPA does not include graduate-level coursework, including master’s or doctoral programs. Only undergraduate coursework completed before the first bachelor’s degree is included.

Does LSAC GPA include community college?

Yes. Community college courses taken for undergraduate credit are included in LSAC GPA calculations.

Does LSAC GPA matter?

Yes. LSAC GPA is one of the most important quantitative factors in law school admissions. Alongside the LSAT score, it strongly influences admissions outcomes and scholarship decisions.

Which is the official LSAC GPA system?

The Credential Assembly Service (CAS) GPA is the only official LSAC GPA system used by ABA-accredited law schools.

Is a 3.8 GPA good for Harvard Law?

Yes. A 3.8 LSAC GPA is highly competitive for Harvard Law School. While admissions are holistic, a GPA at this level places applicants within the academic range of admitted students when paired with a strong LSAT.

Does LSAC GPA count study abroad?

It depends. LSAC includes study-abroad grades if they appear on your home institution transcript with letter grades and credit values. Programs listed only as pass/fail or without grades may be excluded.
