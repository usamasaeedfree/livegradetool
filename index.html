How Does LSAC Calculate GPA?

The Law School Admission Council (LSAC) does not use your university GPA exactly as it appears on your transcript. Instead, LSAC recalculates your undergraduate GPA through the Credential Assembly Service (CAS) to ensure a fair and consistent evaluation for all applicants.


Step 1: Transcript Review

LSAC collects transcripts from every undergraduate institution you attended. All courses completed before your first bachelor’s degree are included in this review.


Step 2: Grade Standardization

Since universities use different grading systems, LSAC converts each grade into a numerical value using its official LSAC Grade Conversion Table. This standardizes all grades to a common 4.0 GPA scale, regardless of the original grading system.


Step 3: Weighted GPA Calculation

Each converted grade is multiplied by its corresponding credit hours. Courses with higher credit hours have a greater impact on your final GPA.


Step 4: Final CAS GPA

LSAC adds all grade points together and divides the total by the number of credit hours. The result is your LSAC CAS GPA, which law schools use to evaluate your application.

This standardized method allows law schools to fairly compare applicants from different universities and education systems. Your LSAC GPA may differ from your university GPA due to variations in grading scales and conversion rules. LSAC applies this process to ensure fairness and consistency across all applicants, regardless of institution, major, or grading system.

LSAC GPA vs. University GPA

Your university GPA is calculated by your institution using its own grading scale and academic policies, which can vary widely between colleges and countries.

Your LSAC GPA, on the other hand, is recalculated by LSAC through the Credential Assembly Service (CAS) using a standardized 4.0 scale.

Because LSAC applies uniform conversion rules to all transcripts, your LSAC GPA may differ from your university GPA. Law schools rely on the LSAC CAS GPA, not your original university GPA, when evaluating applications.
