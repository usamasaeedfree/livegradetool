How Cumulative GPA Is Calculated at LSU

At Louisiana State University (LSU), a student’s cumulative GPA is calculated by combining the grade points earned in all completed courses and dividing them by the total number of attempted credit hours.

Each letter grade is assigned a numerical value, which is then multiplied by the course’s credit hours to determine quality points. The cumulative GPA reflects a student’s overall academic performance across multiple semesters, not just a single term.

LSU Cumulative GPA Formula
Cumulative GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Attempted Credit Hours

Quality points are calculated by multiplying the numerical grade value (such as 4.3 for A+) by the credit hours assigned to each course. All quality points from every semester are added together, then divided by the total credit hours attempted to produce the cumulative GPA.

LSU’s cumulative GPA calculation follows standard university grading practices, but results may be affected by factors such as repeated courses, withdrawals, or transfer credits, depending on how they are recorded on a student’s official transcript.

Factors That Can Affect Your Cumulative GPA at LSU

A student’s cumulative GPA at LSU is influenced by more than just final letter grades. Several academic factors can affect how courses are counted and how quality points are calculated, which in turn impacts the overall GPA shown on an official transcript.

Repeated Courses

When a course is repeated, LSU may apply specific rules regarding how the original and repeated grades are included in the cumulative GPA. Depending on university policy and timing, both attempts or only the most recent grade may be factored into the final GPA calculation.

Withdrawals and Non-Graded Courses

Courses marked as withdrawals or assigned non-graded symbols typically do not contribute grade points, but they may still appear on a student’s academic record. These courses generally do not affect the cumulative GPA unless otherwise specified by LSU policy.

Transfer Credits

Transfer credits accepted by LSU may count toward degree requirements, but they do not always impact the LSU cumulative GPA. In many cases, only courses completed at LSU are included when calculating the official cumulative GPA.

Credit Hour Weight

Courses with higher credit hours have a greater impact on cumulative GPA because they contribute more quality points. Accurately entering credit hours is essential for obtaining a reliable GPA estimate.

LSU Cumulative GPA Calculator FAQs

What is a cumulative GPA at LSU?

A cumulative GPA at LSU represents a student’s overall Grade Point Average across all completed courses that are included in GPA calculations. It combines grade points from multiple semesters rather than reflecting performance in a single term.

What is the difference between semester GPA and cumulative GPA at LSU?

A semester GPA reflects grades earned during a single term, while a cumulative GPA combines results from all semesters included in the academic record. The cumulative GPA provides a broader view of academic performance at LSU.

Do transfer credits affect my cumulative GPA at LSU?

In many cases, transfer credits accepted by LSU count toward degree requirements but do not affect the LSU cumulative GPA. Only courses completed at LSU are typically included in GPA calculations, depending on university policy.

How do repeated courses affect cumulative GPA at LSU?

Repeated courses may be treated differently depending on LSU academic policy. In some situations, both attempts may appear on the transcript, while GPA calculations may prioritize the most recent or highest grade. Students should consult LSU guidelines for specific rules.

Is this LSU GPA calculator official?

No. This calculator is an unofficial estimation tool designed to help students understand their academic standing. Official GPA values are calculated and maintained by Louisiana State University.
